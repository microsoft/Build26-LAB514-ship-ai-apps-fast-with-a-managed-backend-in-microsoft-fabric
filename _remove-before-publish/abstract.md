# Title and Abstract

## Title
From zero to production with Fabric Apps and Copilot CLI

## Refined abstract
Skip the infra plumbing and ship a real enterprise app. In this hands-on lab, build a work-order management app for Contoso DIY's new home services with Fabric Apps—a managed backend giving you database, auth, hosting, functions, storage, and real-time out of the box. Start from a template, deploy to production in one command, then add features through the Copilot CLI without worrying about migrations. Finish by tapping Fabric intelligence to turn app data into insights.

## Draft

Contoso DIY Store is expanding its scope: after you chose the perfect painting for your living room, could get a service pro to do the work for you?

To prepare the day for the field work, we need an app to manage the work orders and assign the right pro to the right job. To build this app, we'll use Fabric Apps, a managed Fabric backend as a service for building enterprise apps.It’s a complete platform for web apps that provides you a backend with managed database, auth, hosting, functions, storage, and real‑time services.

In this lab, we'll see how we can create a Fabric App from a template, deploy it to production in one command, and then use the Copilot CLI to add features without having to worry about the underlying infrastructure or database migrations. We'll finally explore how to bring in Fabric intelligence capabilities to analyze the data generated by our app and get insights from it.
